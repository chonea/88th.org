Section 1. Definitions

  • "Militia" or "88th" refers to this unit in its entirety.
  • "Corps" refers to sub-units within the Militia, each of which at present consists of 11 members.
  • "Lance" refers to fighting squads within each Corps, each of which at present consists of 5 members.
  • "Executive Command" refers to the highest ranking members of the unit, Colonel (10) and Lt. Colonel (9).
  • "Corps Commander" refers to the commander of each sub-unit, or Corps.
  • "Lance Commander" refers to the commander of each lance in a Corps.
  • "Officer" refers to members that hold command positions, i.e. Lieutenant and up.
  • "Commission" refers to the transfer to officer status.

Section 2. Unit Requirements

There are certain prerequisites that must be met in order to be a member of the 88th Mechanized Militia.

  1. Activity

    Each member is expected to maintain a certain level of activity during their tour with the Militia. Unless extenuating circumstances prevent your involvement and you have notified your commander of those circumstances, a reasonable minimum of two (2) evenings per week are expected. This includes meetings, training, game play, and/or tournaments.

  2. MechWarrior® Online™ (MWO)

    You must be a member of the MWO website and have MWO installed on your computer. Your must be able to play MWO with at least minimal hardware/bandwidth requirements. Your software must be kept up-to-date with the latest patches as required for consistent participation with the unit. MWO is a free-to-play online game available for download at mwomercs.com.

  3. Teamspeak

    This unit uses a voice chat software called Teamspeak (teamspeak.com) for in-game organization, direction, and just plain fun. If you have a problem with having your voice heard, do not join this unit.

  4. Equipment

    You must maintain the minimal system requirements for multiplayer play in MechWarrior Online. In addition to the MWO system requirements, a microphone will be required for voice communications.

  5. Registration

    Because of the necessity of team communication both online and offline, you must be registered with this website and our forum. Often these are our primary tools to inform our membership of important happenings and updates.

Section 3. Code of Conduct

The Militia requires that all members adhere to the Code of Conduct (CoC) as posted on the MWO website (mwomercs.com/conduct).

In addition to these guidelines, the following will apply:

  • The Codex, in particular the sections outlined below, must be followed at all times.
  • A violation of the CoC will likely result in demotion, suspension, or even expulsion from the unit. Violations of the CoC require a meeting, including that member's Corps Commander (if applicable) and the Command Staff, to decide the extent of damage to the unit and the fitting reprimand. The meeting may take place either via text chat, voice chat, or via a special forum on the members intranet.

    Divisions of the Militia

    The unit consists of a command lance and 5 divisions referred to as "Corps". These divisions of the Militia are as follows:

    • ALPHA Corps,
    • BRAVO Corps,
    • CHARLIE Corps,
    • DELTA Corps, and
    • ECHO Corps.

    Each Corps is run somewhat INDEPENDENTLY of the others and is expected to be able to hold its own as an individual fighting force. Each is also assigned a "code name", i.e. an unofficial name that reflects that Corps' fighting spirit. Members of each Corps are encouraged to get to know one another, as they will be expected perform together.

    The bulk of each Corps is divided into 2 lances: a "heavy" assault lance and a "light" assault lance. Typically, each lance within a Corps will perform a specific function in battle. These lances are refered to by the Corps designator and a number, for example "BRAVO 1" refers to the BRAVO heavy lance, and "BRAVO 2" refers to the BRAVO light lance. This makes quick tactical decisions in battle possible for those in command.

    Section 5.  COMMAND RESPONSIBILITY

    Rank = Responsibility

    Rank within the Militia is not just defined by a title. With each rank comes a new level of responsibility. This is especially true in regard to command positions, i.e. Lance Command, Corps Command, and Executive Command. Following is a description of each level of responsibility:

    General Ranks
    Sergeant Major (05-SGM), Sergeant (04-SGT), Corporal (03-CPL),
    Private First Class (02-PFC), Private (01-PVT)

    Description: These members are the "big stick" that the Militia carries into battle. They are the base fighting strength of our unit. The general ranks must learn to work well together and under the direction of several different levels of command. Their immediate battle commander is their Lance Commander, either Light (LLC) or Heavy (HLC).

    Definition of Command Responsibility: General ranks do not hold command responsibility with respect to unit structure. When split into smaller fighting units on the field, the highest ranking member assumes command responsibility. If ranks are the same, the member with the highest seniority assumes command. The exception to this is the Sergeant Major (SGM), which, in addition to being a big gun, must train for assumption of duties as an LLC. These ranks are a NORMAL ACTIVITY role in the unit. Members that fall below normal activity will be removed as determined by their CC.

    Light Lance Commander - LLC
    Lieutenant (06-LUT)

    Description: Light Lance Commanders have assembled under them the lightest and fastest Mechs of the unit. The Light Lance of each Corps handles missions that utilize that speed, such as recon missions, fire support for the heavier assault mechs, short-range support for the heavier long-range mechs, or even suprise attacks on opposing Mechs that have wandered away from the main battle group. It is essential that Light Lance members learn to work together and concentrate their efforts, and it is the Light Lance Commander's responsibility to make sure that they do just that. Due to the ranks involved in the Light Lance (PVT and PFC), the Light Lance Commander also assumes the pilot training duties that are essential to the success of the unit.

    Definition of Command Responsibility: Light Lance Commander (LLC), responsible for PILOT TRAINING for and EXECUTION of basic to advanced maneuvers in light mechs. Light lance is a supporting role, meaning they primarily act as either an escort for heavy mechs or as long-range suppression. The combination of a heavy and a light winging in a tight formation is a hard nut to crack. Use of the light lance as long range suppression is needed as the lighter mechs can close those distances quickly and tie up an opponent until the heavies come into range. An LLC is also expected to train for the HLC position, which may occasionally involve assumption of HLC duties for evaluation purposes as determined by the CC. This is a HIGH ACTIVITY role in the unit.


    Heavy Lance Commander - HLC
    Captain (07-CPT)

    Description: Heavy Lance Commanders have assembled under them the medium to heavy class Mechs of the unit. The Heavy Lance of each Coprs handles missions that utilize their strength over their speed. Due to the higher payload of the larger Mechs, their roles in battle are typically full assault or long-range support. As the Heavy Lance pilots are the tactical "gears" of our war machine, thhe Heavy Lance Commander must learn to take advantage of the added weight to give our unit the highest advantage on the battlefield. He must learn to coordinate efforts with the Light Lance Commander to fully round out the firepower of our fighting units.

    Definition of Command Responsibility    : Heavy Lance Commander (HLC), responsible for TACTICAL TRAINING and EXECUTION of advanced maneuvers for heavy armor. The Captain should know the inner working of both the Light Lance and the Heavy Lance, assuming he performs well in both command tasks. Knowledge of Light Lance maneuvers and abilities is essential to his duties as HLC. The HLC will work closely with the LLC to make sure he is getting the support he requires for success on the battlefield. An HLC is also expected to train for the CC position, which may occasionally involve assumption of CC duties for evaluation purposes as determined by the CC. This is a HIGH ACTIVITY role in the unit.

    Corps Commander - CC
    Major (08-MAJ)

    Description: Corps Commanders as a whole are in essence the backbone of the Militia. Each Corps within the Militia is under direct command of one individual. Each Corps Commander must learn to use his two "fists", the Heavy Lance and the Light Lance, to make the most effective presence on the battlefield. He works very closely with his Lance Commanders and is ultimately responsible for their success. Due to this extremely high level of responsibility, all issues within his Corps regarding assignment of rank and delegation of duties are made by him. A Corps Commander must always maintain a high level of alertness in battle and be fully aware of his Corps' performance on the field. He must be quick to change tactics "on the fly", and he must be sure that his Lances understand those tactics and can adapt to them quickly. A Corps' success will most likely hinge on the abilities of its Commander, so only the most organized, most adaptive, most active, and most skilled members of the Militia are considered for this position.

    In addition to battle command, a Corps Commander handles any and all administration of his Corps, including the signing of new members, roster changes, practice schedules, and meetings. A Corps Commander must also be capable of cutting members that do not measure up to the standards of the Militia.

    Definition of Command Responsibility    : Corps Commander (CC), responsible for administration of his Corps, and makes sure everything runs smoothly in both lances. His decisions are final with respect to his Corps only (with the exception of XO and CO). He must make sure that his Corps conforms to the standards set for the unit, so that each Corps knows what to expect from the others. He must also make sure that his officers understand their duties and perform them well. His tactical decisions are of the highest regard in his Corps, although he may leave some room for experimentation on the part of his officers. He is also responsible for inter-Corps correspondance with other CC's for strategical input, training scrimages, or transferance of members for personal, promotional, or tactical reasons. He should be ready to lose an officer (due to promotion, demotion, or expulsion) at any time, and should make sure that the appropriate members have been trained to assume those positions. This is a VERY HIGH ACTIVITY role in the unit.

    Executive Command: Commanding Officer - CO, Executive Officer - XO
    General (GEN), Colonel (COL)

    Description: The Executive Command, is effectively the executive, legislative, and judicial governing elements all rolled into one. Decisions which affect the direction of the Militia as a whole are made by these members. They set unit policy and enforce it through their Corps Commanders. Command Staff members are also responsible for any administration of the unit which does not fall under the role of a Corps Commander. Administration of the Members Intranet is handled by these members as well.

    Ultimately, the Executive Command will oversee all activity of the Militia and monitor its performance as a unit, and do whatever is necessary to make it work.

    Definition of Command Responsibility    : Commanding Officer (CO) and Executive Officer (XO), responsible for administration of the unit as a whole, setting of unit policy, and setting of unit structure. Oversees all operations of the unit, including the inner workings of each Corps for evaluation purposes. At any time the CO or XO may conduct a review of a Corps' operations. The reviewing member will make whatever changes are necessary to bring the Corps up to par with the rest of the unit. Those changes could conceivably involve the replacement of the CC if it is necessary for survival of that Corps. In the most dire of circumstances, the CO or XO may take the upper hand in Corps issues without a full review. This could include recruitment of new Militia members, expulsion of members from the unit, or member transfers from one Corps to another. In any case, the decisions made by the Executive Command are FINAL. These are a VERY HIGH ACTIVITY roles in the unit.


    Section 6.  LEAGUE PARTICIPATION

    MW4 Vengeance League

    BE AWARE of any and all rules that apply during league play. These rules can be found under 'League Rules' on the MW4VL site (http://www.mw4vl.com).

    Mercenaries

    The Militia is a MERCENARY unit, meaning special rules regarding contracts will apply. Make sure these special rules are understood. If necessary, contact a Rules Admin to get a ruling on any issues that may arise.

    As mercenaries, we are capable of taking contracts against other units on either a multi-battle or a per-battle basis. Multi-battle contracts are can be browsed or taken by Command Staff members under the 'Contracts' area of the MW4VL site. In multi-battle contracts, DO NOT DISCLOSE the employer to the target unit, nor should you tell the target that the battle is for fulfillment of the contract. This is in the MW4VL rules and breaks the trust assumed under the contract. In a per-battle contract, at least one member of the employing unit must participate in the battle with you. This means that any members of the Militia may participate in a battle as "filler", as long as a fee percentage has been declared before the battle. If this does not make sense to you, READ THE RULES.

    The MW4VL is a "merc friendly" league, and we are lucky to be in the position that we are. Take advantage of that position as much as possible.


    Section 7.  UNIT PARTICIPATION

    Recognition

    Do you know who the Militia is? Understand our history, learn it, live it. Your experience here will have much more meaning.

    Validation

    Before league participation is allowed, new members must be validated by either the Corps Commander of the recruiting Corps, by that Commander's appointed training officer, or by the Command Staff. The validation process is a training/testing process that is used to verify one's ability to attack, defend, or operate in a team environment effectively. Ideally, validation should occur within the MW4 environment. However, the MW3 environment can be substituted if neccessary. If the validation occurs under MW3, the M3LX cheat detection module is REQUIRED by all individuals involved. Unfortunately, our experience has shown that possible cheats have appeared under testing conditions.

    Training mods are meant to determine one's knowledge of various weapons systems and how to use them effectively. The MW4VL league is Omni-mod, i.e. all weapons systems are allowed, but basic knowledge of each type of system, how it is used, and how it is avoided are the key elements in this process. Team functionality is also an important aspect of the validation process.

    The following basic criteria can be used for most player validations:
    1. Energy weapons only, 1 vs 1 combat;
    2. Projectile weapons only, 1 vs 1 combat;
    3. Missile weapons only, 1 vs 1 combat;
    4. Omni-mod (all weapons), team-based combat.

    Validating officers should not clear a member for league play until it is certain that the validatee understands each of these weapons systems and can function effectively in team combat situations.

    Activity

    Every member of this unit is expected to uphold a reasonable level of activity. A unit with inactive members is a dead unit, as we should all be painfully aware. The minimal activity requirements are as per section 2 above.

    Contact

    Every member of this unit     is expected to maintain a reasonable level of contact with the leaders of the unit. Ranks below Corps Commander are expected to keep their CC's informed of changes in activity. Corps Commanders are expected to keep the CO and XO informed of Corps performance, activity, and roster changes. Communication is the key to a successful team.

    Presentation

    Always put your best foot forward in dealings with players both internal and external to the unit. We wish to present ourselves as serious, competent players, that enjoy working together as a team. Keeping morale up is one thing, but acting the fool is another...be aware of the difference.

    Etiquette

    et·i·quette
    the conduct or procedure prescribed by authority to be observed in social or official life

    A unit is often only as good as the image it presents. Therefore, in formal situations (i.e. league battles, dealings with members from other units, or dealings with league administration), please address officers and officials with the formal "Sir". This incudes members of other units as well as our own.

    This will become particularly involved as we progress into more role-play (RP) situations and/or leagues.

    Dealings within the unit are considered "at ease", and should not require formalities. All members of this unit work hard and deserve respect, or they would not be here. "At ease", however, does not waive awareness of rank. If common courtesies must be waived for you to get your point across to a superior ranking officer, a simple request to speak "freely and privately" should be made. An officer should honor such requests, and should be available to open discussions of this nature.
    Promotion or Demotion

    Promotion within the Militia is based on a number of factors, which include leadership ability, skill level, dedication, attitude, activity, and availability. Each Corps Commander should be aware of each of these factors when considering promotion of one of his Corps members. The squeaky wheel does NOT get the grease here. Promotion within the Command ranks is decided by the Executive Command and follows the same guidelines as any other member.

    Demotions are an unfortunate occurance that are required to continue efficient operation, but should only be used as a last resort. If any member of this Militia is demoted for any reason, an explanation is required for the Command Staff. Demotions within the Command ranks again will be decided by the Executive Command.

    Promotion or demotion of officers (Lieutenant and up) require consultation and APPROVAL of a member of the Executive Command. These are key positions that affect the unit as a whole, and therefore must be handled with care.
    Explusion

    Expulsion is the forced removal of a member from the unit. Every member of this team has been brought on board because we KNOW they will perform. We do not just add people to the roster and see how they work out. Therefore, our members are NOT removed without due process. In order for a Corps Commander to remove a member from the roster, the following must be performed:

    1. Demotion before explusion. This is self-explanatory. A demotion is a warning of responsibilities unfulfilled. The ONLY exception to this is for a violation of the Code of Conduct.

    2. Correspondance. A member that is in danger of being removed from the roster must first be given proper notice of his violation(s) and given a chance to explain or resign. This notice should be made via email and carbon copies of the notice should be sent to the Executive Command. A resonable amount of time (2 to 3 days) should be given for a reply.

    3. If the offending member is an officer, approval. This officer has been given a rank of responsibility in this unit and reasoning for removal must be approved by the Executive Command.

    4. Removal. Only after the above tasks have been performed may a member of this unit be forcefully removed from the roster.

    Abuse of Power

    Abusing the power of command, namely the Corps Commander position, will be dealt with on a case by case basis. Abuse constitutes over-manipulation of the roster for personal issues wth certain Corps members, preventing the advancement of certain deserving individuals due to unfounded personality issues, lack of communication with the Executive Command when making drastic changes to Corps rosters, or manipulation of the Codex in any form without approval of the Executive Command.

    This should not be confused with the finality of decisions made by the Executive Command.

    Continual abuse of command will likely result in a Corps review or demotion to a non-officer position.
